Finca El Paraíso sits on a southwest-facing hillside outside Piendamó, in Cauca. The farm is small by Colombian standards — under 30 hectares of coffee — but the on-site processing infrastructure is unusually elaborate: stainless-steel bioreactors with real-time pH and temperature monitoring, ozone sterilization equipment, a thermal-shock rinse station, and slow-drying beds and parabolic dryers tuned for the long, careful programs the Bermudezes prefer. Most of what the farm grows — Castillo, Gesha, Wush Wush, Sidra — is processed on-site under Diego's direction. The lots that come off this property have set Colombian auction records.

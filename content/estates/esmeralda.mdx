Esmeralda is the oldest of the Arcila family farms outside Armenia, in Quindío. It is one of five (Esmeralda, Villarazo, Santa Mónica, Maracay, Buenos Aires) that Jairo runs alongside his wife and daughter's separate operations. The farm grows mostly Castillo and Caturra at 1,500–1,850 meters, with named-variety blocks of Gesha, Pink Bourbon, Chiroso, Java, Papayo, and Sidra added over the past decade. Processing is done on-site by Jairo and his daughter Marta, with a comparatively restrained aesthetic — longer washed dwells, careful naturals, and the family's "Ice fermentation" cold-anaerobic program. Lots ship through the family export company, Cofinet.

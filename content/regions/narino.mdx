Nariño is the southernmost coffee department, sharing a border with Ecuador and centered around Pasto and the Galeras volcano. Coffee here grows at unusually high altitudes — most named lots come from 1,800 to 2,300 meters — and the dramatic day-night temperature swings produce coffees with notable acidity and density.

Pablo Guerrero's Finca El Obraje sits in Tangua, west of Pasto. The region is also home to processors helping smallholders with microlot fermentation, including Eyder Andrés Martínez, whose work shows up in lots from neighboring producers like Daiver Mallama.

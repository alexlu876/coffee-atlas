Cauca is the southern Andean department where, more than anywhere else, the modern Colombian fermentation school took shape. The Piendamó–La Sierra–Popayán corridor has the highest density of producers running thermal-shock and bioreactor processing; Diego Bermudez's Finca El Paraíso and Wilton Benitez's Granja Paraíso 92 are the two most-cited names, but a long tail of smaller producers learn from and iterate on each other's work.

Coffee grows here between roughly 1,500 and 2,100 meters, with the highest altitudes in Inzá to the east. Cauca's reputation is built on Castillo, Gesha, Pink Bourbon, Wush Wush, and a handful of more exotic varieties — and increasingly, on what producers are doing during the four to ten days between cherry and parchment.

Valle del Cauca runs from the Pacific coast up the western Andes east of Cali. Coffee here concentrates in the Caicedonia–Sevilla corridor at the northern edge of the department, where Café Granja La Esperanza — Rigoberto and Luis Eduardo Herrera's farm — has been producing competition-grade lots since the 1990s. They were among the first commercial Gesha planters in Colombia.

Altitudes run 1,300–1,900 meters. The region's reputation rests more on consistency at the elite tier than on the kind of public experimentation visible elsewhere in the cluster.

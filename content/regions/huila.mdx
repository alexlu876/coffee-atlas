Huila is Colombia's largest coffee-producing department by volume, and historically its most consistent. The valleys around Pitalito, Bruselas, and Acevedo have always done washed Caturra and Castillo with quiet competence; the past decade has added a layer of microlot experimentation, especially in the Macizo Colombiano around Pitalito.

Altitudes run from 1,200 meters in the lower valleys to over 2,000 in the southern hills near the Cauca border. Bimodal harvests; primarily washed processes, with naturals and anaerobic styles becoming more common at the specialty tier.
